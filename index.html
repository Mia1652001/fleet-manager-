The problem

Spreadsheets and notebooks lose money quietly.

Most small rental companies run on a shared spreadsheet, a wall planner, or someone's memory. It works until two customers are promised the same car.

Without a system

  • Double bookings discovered at the counter
  • Cars returned late that nobody is tracking
  • Invoices calculated by hand, sometimes forgotten
  • No record of who rented what, or when
  • Servicing remembered only when something breaks

With Fleet Manager

  • Double bookings are refused before they happen
  • Overdue rentals flagged automatically
  • Invoices calculated from the daily rate
  • Full rental history for every customer
  • Service reminders by date or by mileage
What it does

Everything a small rental company actually needs.

No GPS trackers, no telematics, no installation, no training course. Five screens that cover the working day.

Fleet

Every car with its live status — available, rented, overdue or out of service — plus daily, weekly and monthly rates.

Bookings

A month calendar of every reservation. The same car can't be booked twice, and late returns are flagged automatically.

Customers

A register of renters with contact details, licence number and full rental history — built up as you take bookings.

Billing

Invoices calculated from days × daily rate, with advance payments and refundable security deposits tracked separately.

Maintenance

Service schedules by date or mileage, with warnings before they fall due. Cars in the workshop can't be rented by mistake.

Your data, exportable

Download everything as a spreadsheet or a full backup whenever you want. Your records stay yours.

Getting started

There's nothing to download.

Fleet Manager runs in the browser, so it works on whatever devices you already have. Add it to your home screen and it behaves like any other app — full screen, its own icon.

01

On iPhone or iPad

Open the app in Safari, tap the Share button, then choose "Add to Home Screen".

02

On Android

Open the app in Chrome, tap the three-dot menu, then choose "Install app".

03

On a computer

Open the app in Chrome and click the install icon in the address bar — or simply bookmark it.
